Week 4: Microcontroller Programming


This week's assignment was to program an Arduino board to do something. After watching some videos using a micrcocontroller to produce sound using a Piezo buzzer, I really wanted to try and create the Shepard tone. I figured out I would need 4 buzzers after looking at this diagram, but it seemed like I wouldn't have time to get these in time. A friend who owned an Arduino kit found a Piezo buzzer, which I got really excited about, but I couldn't figure out how to get it to sound. It also had no polarity, which I thought was weird.

Still wanting to do something music related, I had the idea of visualizing music through LEDs. I would assign 1 LED to a particular note, and they would light up if a note was "played." The song I had in mind for this project was Bach's Toccata and Fugue in D Minor (the really famous one with the ominous beginning).

After watching some videos demonstrating how to use multiple LEDs, I tried it out with 3, and then added 3 more while also manipulating the on/off and the delays to make it look like the beginning of the Toccata.
